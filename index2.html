Right to equality (Article 14)

Women have the right to equality under the Constitution of India and are entitled to equal treatment and opportunities as men in all spheres of life.

Right to education (Article 21A)

Women have the right to education and the government is responsible for providing free and compulsory education to all children between the ages of 6 and 14.

Right to work (Article 39(d))

Women have the right to work and to receive equal pay for equal work.

Right to vote (Article 326)

Women have the right to vote and to participate in the political process.

Right to property (Article 15)

Women have the right to own and inherit property and to control and manage their own property.

Right to health (Article 47)

Women have the right to access to healthcare services and to receive the best possible medical treatment.

Right to freedom from discrimination (Article 15)

Women have the right to freedom from discrimination on the basis of sex, race, religion, or any other grounds.

Right to freedom from violence (Article 21)

Women have the right to freedom from violence, including domestic violence, sexual assault, and harassment.

Right to freedom of expression (Article 19(1)(a))

Women have the right to freedom of expression, including the right to express their opinions and beliefs freely.

Right to privacy (Article 21)

Women have the right to privacy and to make decisions about their own bodies and health.

Right to marry (Article 21)

Women have the right to marry a person of their choice and to enter into a marriage on the basis of mutual consent.

Right to divorce (Section 13 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955)

Women have the right to seek a divorce if their marriage is no longer working.

Right to participate in cultural and religious activities (Article 25)

Women have the right to participate in cultural and religious activities of their choice.

Right to obtain a passport (Article 19(1)(d))

Women have the right to obtain a passport and to travel freely.

Right to access to justice (Article 21)

Women have the right to access to justice and to seek legal remedies for violations of their rights.

Right to receive compensation for violations of rights (Article 39(a))

Women have the right to receive compensation for violations of their rights, including compensation for harm suffered as a result of violence or discrimination.

Right to form and join organizations (Article 19(1)(c))

Women have the right to form and join organizations, including women's rights organizations and trade unions.

Right to receive support for children (Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973)

Women have the right to receive support for their children, including child support and custody.

Right to participate in decision-making (Article 243D)

Women have the right to participate in decision-making at all levels, including in the home, community, and at the national level.

Right to receive support in old age (Article 41)

Women have the right to receive support and care in old age, including pension and other forms of social security

Women Amendments✒️

The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013

This amendment broadened the definition of sexual assault and provided for stricter punishment for crimes such as rape, sexual harassment, and stalking.

The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012

This act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the protection of children from sexual abuse and exploitation.

The Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986

This amendment strengthened the provisions of the original Dowry Prohibition Act of 1961 and provided for stricter penalties for those who engage in the practice of giving or taking dowry.

The Domestic Violence Act, 2005

This act provides for protection and relief to victims of domestic violence and provides for the punishment of those who engage in acts of domestic violence.

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013

This act provides for the prevention, prohibition, and redressal of sexual harassment of women at the workplace

The Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005

This amendment gave Hindu women equal rights to inherit property as men.

The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019

This act provides for the protection of rights of married Muslim women and makes instant triple talaq (a form of Islamic divorce) illegal.

The Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017

This amendment increased the maternity leave entitlement for women in the organized sector from 12 weeks to 26 weeks.

The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006

This act sets the minimum age of marriage for girls at 18 years and for boys at 21 years, and provides for strict penalties for those who engage in child marriage.

The Representation of the People (Amendment) Act, 2010

This amendment provides for the reservation of one-third of the seats in the local self-government institutions for women.

The National Commission for Women Act, 1990

This act established the National Commission for Women, which is responsible for the promotion and protection of the rights of women.

Bills for Women📜

The Women's Reservation Bill

This bill proposes to reserve 33% of the seats in the Indian Parliament and state legislative assemblies for women. The bill has been introduced several times in the past but is yet to be passed into law.

The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Bill, 2019

This bill was passed by the Indian Parliament and made instant triple talaq (a form of Islamic divorce) illegal.

The POCSO (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences) Amendment Bill, 2019

This bill proposed to make the punishment for sexual offences against children more stringent, and was passed by the Indian Parliament.

The Women's Economic Empowerment Bill

This bill proposes to provide financial and non-financial support to women entrepreneurs and to promote the participation of women in the workforce.

The Prevention of Sexual Harassment at the Workplace Bill

This bill proposes to establish a mechanism for the prevention of sexual harassment of women at the workplace and to provide for the protection of the rights of women employees.


Government Schemes 📄




Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme

Launched on 22nd January 2015 by the Prime Minister of India in Haryana, the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme ensures survival, protection and education of girl children. The scheme aims to address issues of the declining sex ratio over the past few years, create social awareness and enhance the efficiency of welfare services developed for girls.


Who Are the Beneficiaries of This Scheme?
The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme is applicable to the whole country. However, to ensure maximum reach, this scheme is divided into 3 groups. These are -

  1. Primary Group (young and married couples, pregnant mothers and parents)
  2. Secondary Groups (the youth of India, adolescents, in-laws, doctors, private hospitals, nursing homes, diagnostic centres)
  3. Tertiary Groups (general people of the country, religious leaders, voluntary organisations, frontline workers, officials, media and women SHGs Sector)

What Is the Application Process to Avail This Scheme?

  1. Step-1- Visit the registered bank or post office.
  2. Step-2- Collect and fill in the application form of the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme.
  3. Step-3- Attach the required documents and submit the application form.





Working Women Hostel

To promote safe accommodation and environment for working women and provide daycare facilities for their children, the Government of India has introduced the 'Working Women Hostel Scheme'. Through this women empowerment scheme, the Government provides grant-in-aid for construction and new hostel buildings and extension of an existing building in rented premises.


Who Are the Beneficiaries of This Scheme?

  1. Working Women (single, widowed, married, divorced, separated).
  2. This scheme provides a particular preference for working women belonging to the disadvantaged sections of society.
  3. The Scheme guidelines also provide for the reservation of seats for physically challenged beneficiaries.

What Is the Application Process to Avail This Scheme?

  1. Step-1: Approach the WCD department of your concerned state.
  2. Step-2: Collect the application form and fill it in with the correct details.
  3. Step-3: Submit the application form and other essential documents.
             




       

One Stop Centre Scheme

Next in the list of women empowerment schemes in India comes One Stop Centre Scheme. It is a centrally sponsored scheme and is funded through the Nirbhaya fund. State governments receive 100% central assistance to protect women affected by violence (gender-based such as acid attacks, rape, and sexual harassment) in public and private spaces. This scheme facilitates emergency (medical), legal aid and counselling, non-emergency services under one roof to combat all forms of violence against women.


Who Are the Beneficiaries of This Scheme?
All women affected by violence, irrespective of class, caste, region, religion, marital status or sexual orientation, can get benefits under the One Stop Centre Scheme.


What Is the Application Process to Avail This Scheme?
Since this is a different kind of scheme (which provides shelter, medical assistance, etc.), there is no such specific application process.However, women affected by violence can reach for help by any of the following methods:

  1. By communicating by own self
  2. Through women helpline and other emergency response helpline
  3. Through any person, i.e., public servant (as defined under section 21 of Indian Penal Code, 1860), friend, NGO, relative, volunteer

Once the complaint is registered (be it SMS or internet), a text message will reach DPO/PO/ CDPO DYSP /CMO /SHO/ DM/ SP/PO of the district/area as per requirement. Also, if the victim comes in person to register an application or any other person comes on her behalf, the case details will be updated in a system, and a Unique ID will be generated.





Women Helpline Scheme

Women Helpline Scheme is one of the government schemes for women empowerment that intends to give 24x7 emergency responses to women affected by violence in private or public spaces. The universalisation of women helpline numbers has been done in every State and Union Territory through a single toll-free number (181) that provides immediate support to women nationwide. Further, this scheme creates awareness about women empowerment schemes and programs.


Who Are the Beneficiaries of This Scheme?
Any women or girls facing violence or willing to know about various women related schemes or programs


What Is the Application Process to Avail This Scheme?
Women suffering from violence in public and private spaces can contact for instant help or rescue through the following ways:

  1. Step-1: Telephone (mobile phones through calls, mobile apps and fax messages, SMS/text messaging, landlines
  2. Step-2: Internet (emails, social networking sites such as web page, Facebook, Twitter, MyGov.in, etc. web-posts, web-interface)

In this service, a victim's number is tracked or located. If a call gets disconnected or interrupted while a victim is stating her issue due to being sick or disabled, emergency services will reach there (by tracking address) immediately.






Mahila E-Haat

Mahila E-Haat is an initiative launched by the Ministry of Women and Child Development. It is one of the women empowerment schemes in India which provides an opportunity for women entrepreneurs for using technology and presents their products (made/manufactured/sold) on an online platform. With only mobile and internet connections, women entrepreneurs can showcase their products along with descriptions and photographs. Here, buyers can also reach sellers telephonically, physically, through email or any other medium. The list of products may include clothing, fashion accessories, pottery, boxes, home décor, toys and many other things. This initiative supports the 'Make in India' program through an online platform.


Who Are the Beneficiaries of This Scheme? Women entrepreneurs, women self-help groups (SHG), NGOs


What Is the Application Process to Avail This Scheme?

  1. Step-1: Visit the official website of Mahila E-Haat
  2. Step-2: Click on 'Join Us'.
  3. Step-3: A new webpage, i.e., Mahila E-Haat initiative registration will open. Fill in the registration form with correct details and submit it to complete the application process.
